Kootenai Creek - Climbing Route Notes
Last updated: September 1, 2026

Main crag area - lower tier:

Access: Park at the Kootenai Creek trailhead. Walk in about 25 min on the trail.
Take the boot path right at the boulder field. Crag is obvious from there.

The approach is well-worn but muddy after rain. Early October can be slick
on the lower section. Approach shoes sufficient. Not worth bringing technical
footwear for the walk-in.

Route notes from last few sessions:

Left Wall:
  Good warmup routes on the left side. 5.9 to 5.10b range. Crimpy on the
  upper half. Brush off the holds after rain. Rock dries fast if the sun
  hits it by mid-morning.

Center crack system:
  5.10d to 5.11a depending on how you read the sequence. Neil and I have
  been working the crux move on the right-trending crack. Hand jam near
  the bulge is the key hold. Feet smear on the left wall.

Upper tier:
  Only been up twice. Longer approach scramble. Gear route, not sport.
  Takes small cams in the 0.4 to 1 inch range. Bring extra long runners
  for the roof section.

Josie's beta for the 5.11c (right side):
  Skip the obvious hold at the third bolt. Reach past it to the sidepull.
  Rockover with the left foot high. Commit to the move. That's it.

Gear to bring:
  Draws (12 minimum for sport routes)
  60m rope works for everything on the lower tier
  Two cams (0.5 and 0.75) if going to upper tier
  Water (no water source at crag)
  Sun protection - afternoon sun hits the wall directly after 1 PM

Best conditions: morning light until about noon. Leave by 2 if it gets hot.
October window usually good through mid-month.
