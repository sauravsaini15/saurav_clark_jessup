Podcast notes - backcountry and environmental series
Updated September 25, 2026

Podcasts in the rotation right now:

Dirtbag Diaries (ongoing):
  Good on the road. Stories about people who prioritize the outdoors
  over conventional life choices. Some episodes feel performative but
  the good ones are honest about cost and consequence. The episode
  about the couple who sold everything to ski patrol in Idaho was solid.

The Wild podcast (sporadic):
  Longer form. Environmental journalism focus. Recent episode on
  wildfire corridor management in the northern Rockies was dense but
  worth it. Covers the political side more than I usually want but
  the field science parts are good.

Outside podcast:
  Mixed. Too much gear talk. Skip the product episodes. The ones with
  researchers or land managers are worth the time.

Notes from a recent episode (The Wild, September 15):
  Interview with a ranger and a hydrologist on trail corridor drainage
  failure in the Cascades. Points made:
  - Drainage design from the 1980s not scaled for current precipitation
  - Most failures visible in photos years before they became incidents
  - Budget constraints pushed documentation over maintenance
  - The argument for photo-based condition documentation as an early
    warning system was interesting. More useful than written notes
    for picking up gradual change over seasons.

Other notes:
  There was a Jason Isbell interview somewhere - cannot remember which
  show. He talked about writing songs about places before they change
  instead of after. I liked that idea.
