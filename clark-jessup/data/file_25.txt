Reading notes - Braiding Sweetgrass, Robin Wall Kimmerer
Started September 2026

Chapter notes (in progress):

Prologue / Skywoman Falling:
  The idea that the world was built on a gift - Skywoman carries seeds
  and land emerges from what she brings. Kimmerer sets up the contrast
  between the Skywoman story and the Genesis story (earth as given vs
  earth as burden after Fall). The difference in relationship with land
  feels important for the work I do.

Asters and Goldenrod:
  The question of why those two species bloom together in that specific
  color combination. Kimmerer's professor dismisses it as aesthetics
  unworthy of science. She finds her way to answer it as a real
  question anyway - pollinator signaling, visual contrast for bees.
  The idea that "beauty is itself an argument" stuck with me.

Picking Sweetgrass:
  The protocol of taking only what you need and never more than half.
  The reciprocity idea. Came up in my interpretive work - how to talk
  about resource limits without making it feel like a rule. This chapter
  is closer to the right framing.

Allegiance to Gratitude:
  Haudenosaunee Thanksgiving Address. Every element named and given
  thanks before anything else happens. I keep thinking about how visitor
  briefings work versus this. Most of what I do is list rules. This
  suggests a different order - start with what is good and worth
  protecting, then go to how we protect it.
