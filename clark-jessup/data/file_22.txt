Ski Trail Notes - Glacier area and surrounding corridors
Updated: March 10, 2026

Notes from this season. Mostly cross-country and backcountry access.

Highline connector (upper):
  Good coverage through February, patchy March. Aspect is north-facing
  so it holds later than the south approaches. Take the left fork at the
  junction above the parking area. The right fork got cut by a wind
  event in January - blowdown still there as of February.

North Fork road corridor:
  Best early season access when the lower trails are icy or bare.
  Skied it twice in December and once in January. Flat enough for
  a recovery day. Traffic is low on weekdays.

Going-to-the-Sun approach (lower section, closed in season):
  Off-season access only when road is closed. Check closure dates.
  December through late March typically clear for ski touring.
  Watch for ice on the shaded curves.

Lake McDonald basin (east end):
  Solid coverage from mid-December most years. The trail around the
  east shore stays clear of debris. Snowshoers use the same trail
  so conditions vary based on traffic.

Gear notes:
  Wax skis still working for most conditions this season. Klister in
  March when the freeze-thaw cycle starts. Boots are getting stiff -
  add to gear check list before next season.

Weather pattern this winter:
  La Nina year. Below average snow through January. February came in
  strong. March has been variable. Overall decent but not exceptional.
